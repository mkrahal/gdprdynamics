Frequently Asked Questions

What is legal basis for processing?

EU GDPR recognizes six legitimate reasons which allow merchants to process customer data, at least one of these must apply to your situation whenever you process customer personal data.

Consent: the customer has granted consent for you to process their personal data. This would be the most likely legal basis for most of your store's data processing activities relating to marketing.

Contract: the processing is necessary for the performance of a contract. In this context, a contract refers to any event where there is an element of exchange (such as the exchange of goods or services for money). This would be the most likely legal basis for most of your store's data processing activities relating to sales processing.

Legal obligation: the processing is necessary to ensure compliance with legal obligations.

Public interests: the processing is necessary to ensure public interests (ex. fraud detection).

Legitimate interests: the processing is necessary to ensure the interests of the merchant or thrid parties.

Vital interests: the processing is necessary to ensure individual safety.

What are the different event types?

Data Consent: store visitor has accepted the GDPR data policy terms and conditions.

Data Collection: customer personal data has been collected and registered in shop database.

Data Rectification: customer personal data has been modified by the customer or the store owner.

Data Access Request: customer data access request has been recieved and processed.

Data Removal Request: customer data removal request has been recieved.

Data Removal Request Queued: customer data removal request has been queued for processing pending termination of the charge-back period.

Data Removal Request Processed: customer removal request has been processed following the termination of the charge-back period.
